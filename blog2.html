Decoding College Admissions: Your Ultimate Guide to Success

The Common App is a critical part of your application. Ensure that your profile information is accurate and consistent across all documents. When detailing your education, verify your transcript details and highlight any advanced coursework or unique programs you’ve participated in. In the activities section, prioritize your most significant involvements and use active verbs to describe your achievements. Your essays are your chance to shine; be authentic in your personal statement and tailor each supplemental essay to reflect your fit with the specific college.

"Preparing for the SAT or ACT requires careful planning. Start by taking diagnostic tests to identify your strengths and weaknesses, then create a focused study plan. Use a variety of resources such as prep books, online tools, and courses if possible. On test day, manage your time efficiently and be familiar with the test format to avoid surprises. If your first attempt doesn’t reflect your potential, analyze your performance and focus on areas for improvement before retaking the test."

Vedant Harlanka (UofT)

When it comes to activities and awards, depth is more important than breadth. Emphasize a few activities where you’ve made significant contributions rather than listing many minor involvements. Highlight any leadership roles and the impact of your participation. For awards, list those that are most relevant and significant, and provide context for lesser-known recognitions to explain their importance and the competition involved.

Overall, approach the admissions process with thorough research and organization. Identify colleges that align with your academic interests, career goals, and personal values. Understand their specific requirements and deadlines. Maintain a timeline and checklist for application tasks to stay on track. Choose recommenders who know you well and provide them with a summary of your accomplishments to aid in writing strong recommendation letters. For interviews, prepare by researching the college, practicing common questions, and dressing appropriately.
